# Go-to-Market Strategy (GTM)

## Who is the exact target user?

Our target user is a **Head of Engineering** or **CTO** at a Series A/B funded SaaS startup (50-200 employees) that heavily leverages AI tools in their development workflow. They are typically overwhelmed with managing cloud spend and are looking for actionable insights to optimize their AI infrastructure costs without compromising developer productivity. They are early adopters of new technologies and are active in developer communities.

## What they Google or scroll through right before they'd want this tool?

*   "How to reduce OpenAI API costs"
*   "AI tool spend optimization"
*   "Cloud cost management for AI startups"
*   "Best AI coding assistants for teams"
*   "Comparing Claude vs. GPT-4 pricing"
*   "Hidden costs of AI development"
*   "FinOps for AI"

## Where they hang out online (specific subreddits, Slack groups, Discord servers, X lists)?

*   **Subreddits:** `r/ExperiencedDevs`, `r/SaaS`, `r/FinOps`, `r/MachineLearning`, `r/openai`, `r/aws`
*   **Slack Groups:** "Tech Leaders" (invite-only), "FinOps Foundation" community Slack, various startup founder/CTO Slack communities.
*   **Discord Servers:** Communities around popular AI tools (e.g., OpenAI Discord, Anthropic Discord), developer communities focused on cloud infrastructure.
*   **X (formerly Twitter) Lists:** Following prominent VCs, AI researchers, FinOps experts, and startup founders.

## How you'd get the first 100 users in 30 days with $0 paid budget?

1.  **Targeted LinkedIn Outreach (30 users):** Identify 500+ relevant Head of Engineering/CTOs on LinkedIn. Craft personalized messages highlighting the pain point of AI overspend and offering the tool as a free solution. Focus on genuine connection, not spam.
2.  **Guest Posts/Community Engagement (30 users):** Write 2-3 high-quality guest posts for popular developer blogs (e.g., The New Stack, InfoWorld, freeCodeCamp) or FinOps blogs, showcasing the tool as a practical solution. Actively participate in relevant Reddit/Slack/Discord discussions, offering genuine advice and subtly mentioning the tool where appropriate.
3.  **Product Hunt Launch (20 users):** Prepare a compelling Product Hunt launch with a clear value proposition and engaging visuals. Leverage personal networks to upvote and comment, driving initial visibility.
4.  **"Show HN" (10 users):** Post a concise and problem-focused "Show HN" on Hacker News, explaining the tool's purpose and inviting feedback.
5.  **Direct Email to Early Adopter Lists (10 users):** Leverage existing networks or publicly available lists of early-stage startup founders/CTOs (e.g., from accelerators, incubators) with a personalized email explaining the tool's value.

## The unfair distribution channel — what's the one thing only you (or Credex) could do?

Credex's unfair advantage is its **direct access to discounted AI infrastructure credits**. This tool, by surfacing real overspend, creates a natural funnel for Credex's core business. The audit itself is the lead magnet, and for high-savings cases, Credex can directly offer a solution that competitors cannot: tangible, discounted credits. This transforms a free audit into a direct sales opportunity, making the tool a powerful lead-generation engine.

## What week-1 traction looks like if this works (specific numbers)?

*   **500 unique visitors** to the audit tool.
*   **100 completed audits** (users who submit the form and see results).
*   **20 leads captured** (users who provide email after seeing significant savings).
*   **5 high-savings cases identified** (>$500/month potential savings).
*   **10 social shares** of audit results (via the shareable URL).
