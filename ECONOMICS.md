# Unit Economics

If Credex deployed this tool tomorrow, here's a breakdown of the unit economics:

## What's a converted lead worth to Credex (estimate; show your reasoning)?

**Estimated Value of a Converted Lead: $5,000 - $15,000 ARR**

**Reasoning:**
*   **Average Customer Profile:** We assume a converted lead is a SaaS startup (50-200 employees) with significant AI spend.
*   **Credex's Offering:** Credex offers discounted AI infrastructure credits. A typical enterprise deal for AI credits could range from $50,000 to $200,000+ annually.
*   **Discount Margin:** If Credex captures even a small percentage (e.g., 10-20%) of the customer's annual AI spend through their discounted credits, the ARR generated per customer is substantial.
*   **Conservative Estimate:** Taking a conservative approach, if a customer saves $500/month (a high-savings case identified by the tool), that's $6,000/year. If Credex captures a portion of that saving, or becomes their primary AI credit provider, the ARR could easily be in the $5,000 - $15,000 range for a single converted lead in the first year, with potential for expansion.

**Overall CAC for the tool (initial acquisition): Effectively $0** as it leverages organic, time-based efforts. The tool itself is the lead generation mechanism.

## What conversion rate from “audit completed” → “Credex consultation booked” → "credit purchase” makes this profitable?

Let's assume:
*   **Audit Completed → Lead Captured (Email Opt-in):** 20% (from GTM plan: 100 completed audits -> 20 leads captured).
*   **Lead Captured → Credex Consultation Booked:** 10% (for high-savings cases).
*   **Credex Consultation Booked → Credit Purchase:** 20% (assuming a strong sales process for qualified leads).

**Overall Conversion Rate (Audit Completed to Credit Purchase):** 20% * 10% * 20% = **0.4%**

**Profitability Analysis:**
If 100 completed audits yield 0.4 credit purchases, and each purchase is worth $5,000 ARR, then 100 audits generate $2,000 ARR. This is profitable given the $0 CAC for initial acquisition. The key is scaling the "audit completed" volume.

## What would have to be true for this tool to drive $1M ARR in 18 months?

To drive $1M ARR in 18 months, with an average converted lead value of $10,000 ARR (mid-range estimate) and an overall conversion rate of 0.4% (Audit Completed to Credit Purchase):

*   **Number of Converted Leads Needed:** $1,000,000 / $10,000 = 100 converted leads.
*   **Number of Audit Completions Needed:** 100 converted leads / 0.004 (conversion rate) = **25,000 audit completions.**

**What would have to be true:**

1.  **High Virality/Shareability:** The tool must be inherently shareable, with users actively promoting their savings reports. This would drive down the effective CAC even further and significantly increase the "audit completed" volume.
2.  **Consistent High-Quality Traffic:** Credex needs to consistently drive high-quality traffic (Heads of Eng/CTOs) to the tool through content marketing, partnerships, and community engagement.
3.  **Strong Sales Follow-up:** A highly efficient sales team must be in place to convert the identified high-savings leads into paying Credex customers.
4.  **Expanding AI Spend Market:** The overall market for AI tools and associated spend must continue to grow rapidly, ensuring a large pool of potential customers.
5.  **Tool Accuracy & Trust:** The audit logic must remain highly accurate and defensible, building trust with users and encouraging them to share their results and engage with Credex.

## Show the math even if your inputs are rough estimates. Approximate numbers > no numbers.

All calculations above use approximate numbers and conservative estimates. The core assumption is that the tool effectively identifies a pain point (AI overspend) and Credex offers a compelling solution (discounted credits). The profitability hinges on the tool's ability to generate a high volume of qualified leads at a very low (ideally $0) acquisition cost.
