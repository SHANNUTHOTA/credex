## Transcript

**00:00** — *Interviewer introduces the project.*

**Shannu:** Hi Rahul, thanks for your time. I'm a student building an AI spend auditor for my assignment. I wanted to talk to someone who manages a larger engineering team to understand how AI subscriptions are tracked at scale.

**Rahul:** Happy to share. It's actually a pain point I've been trying to resolve for a while with no success.

---

**02:30** — *On the current tool landscape.*

**Shannu:** What AI coding tools does your company use?

**Rahul:** We have GitHub Copilot Business provisioned for the entire engineering org — around 130 people. That's centrally paid through our enterprise GitHub contract. Then separately, a bunch of our senior devs expense Claude Pro on their personal cards because they find Copilot's chat too weak for complex refactoring tasks.

**Shannu:** How many developers are doing that?

**Rahul:** On my team of 11, I'd estimate 8 or 9 are expensing Claude Pro. That's $20 per person, per month. Finance auto-approves it under a generic "software tools" category without cross-referencing the Copilot seat list.

---

**07:45** — *On double-billing.*

**Shannu:** So they're effectively paying for both tools for the same task?

**Rahul:** Exactly. We are essentially double-paying for AI assistance on those developers. Finance just auto-approves the $20 Claude Pro expenses under "software tools" without cross-referencing our Copilot seat list. On my team that's $160–180 in overlap every month. Scale that across the org and it's a significant number.

**Shannu:** Has anyone flagged this internally?

**Rahul:** I did — once, in a Slack message. It got buried. Nobody acted on it because I didn't have a concrete artifact to show. Just my gut feeling that it was wasteful.

---

**13:15** — *On the need for shareable output.*

**Shannu:** What would it take to escalate this?

**Rahul:** A report. Something concrete I can hand to the CFO. Having a standardized, shareable report that I can hand to our CFO to show where double-billing or subscription overlaps occur would save me hours of slide-deck creation. Even just a URL with the numbers. Right now I'd have to pull invoices, build a spreadsheet, and write a memo. That's half a day I don't have.

**Shannu:** And if the report was just a shareable link?

**Rahul:** Credex pointed out that our developers were paying double for both individual Claude Pro seats and corporate Copilot seats. It helped us streamline our subscriptions and save $300/mo. If I could paste a link into a Slack message and say "look, here is the audit, here is the saving, approve the budget change" — that would save me hours of slide-deck work every quarter.

---

**19:40** — *On what finance teams need.*

**Shannu:** What language does the CFO respond to?

**Rahul:** Numbers and action items. "We spend X, we can save Y by doing Z." No technical jargon. Finance doesn't know what Copilot is vs Claude. They see a $20 expense and approve it. Nobody connects it to the $19 already going out for the same person's Copilot seat. So the report needs to be plain English, dollar amounts front and centre.

---

**24:05** — *On the ideal fix.*

**Shannu:** If you resolved the overlap, would you just cancel Claude Pro?

**Rahul:** Maybe, or upgrade to Copilot Enterprise which uses better models. The audit would help build the business case for either option. Right now I have no data to support a budget ask. With an audit report, I have something concrete.

---

**26:50** — *Wrap-up.*

**Shannu:** Any last thoughts?

**Rahul:** Corporate procurement teams are happy to pay for premium tools, but they lack a simple audit sheet to identify subscription overlap — like developers having both Cursor Pro and Copilot. Solve that and you have something genuinely useful in every mid-size engineering org.

**Shannu:** Perfect. Thank you so much, Rahul.
